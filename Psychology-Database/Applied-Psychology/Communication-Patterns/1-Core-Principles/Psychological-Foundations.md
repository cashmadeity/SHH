# Psychological Foundations of Communication

## Self-Determination Theory (SDT)

**Source**: Deci & Ryan (validated by NIH, extensively cited in motivation research)

### Three Core Needs
1. **Autonomy** - Sense of control and choice
   - People respond better to messaging that preserves agency
   - **Application**: "You can choose to..." > "You must..."
   - **Games**: Open-ended objectives, branching paths
   - **Therapy**: Collaborative goal-setting, autonomy support

2. **Competence** - Capability and skill growth
   - Need to see progress and mastery
   - **Application**: Clear metrics, visible advancement, challenge calibration
   - **Games**: Skill trees, difficulty curves, achievement systems
   - **Marketing**: "Join 50,000 successful users" (competence modeling)

3. **Relatedness** - Connection to others
   - Fundamental human need for belonging
   - **Application**: Community language, shared identity, social proof
   - **Games**: Guilds, multiplayer, shared challenges
   - **Therapy**: Therapeutic alliance, validation, normalization

## Social Proof (Cialdini, validated by CDC communication research)

### Types and Applications

| Type | Mechanism | Application |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Expert Proof** | Authority figures validate message | Therapist recommendations, medical messaging |
| **Peer Proof** | Similar others confirm behavior | "82% of players joined guilds," testimonials |
| **Wisdom of Crowds** | Consensus makes message credible | User reviews, community growth metrics |
| **Scarcity Proof** | Limited availability increases perceived value | Limited-time skins, exclusive groups |

### Usage Guidelines
- **Honest only**: Fabricated proof destroys trust (especially in therapy, marketing)
- **Specific proof > vague**: "1,847 successful clients" > "thousands of people"
- **Relevant to audience**: Match proof to target segment (peers, experts they respect)

## Authority Bias

**How it works**: People trust and follow communicators perceived as credible and expert.

### Ethical Authority Use
✅ **Do**:
- Establish credentials transparently
- Demonstrate knowledge through actionable advice
- Admit limitations and uncertainty
- Use authority to educate, not manipulate

❌ **Don't**:
- Fake credentials (destroys credibility permanently)
- Use authority to bypass informed consent
- Claim expertise outside your domain
- Hide conflicts of interest

### Domain Applications
- **Games**: NPCs with clear expertise (quest-giver, guide)
- **Marketing**: Expert testimonials, research citations
- **Therapy**: Therapist credibility establishes safety and alliance

## Reciprocity

**Principle**: People feel obligated to return favors and match communication styles.

### Mechanisms
1. **Give value first** - Free resources, advice, support
2. **Establish goodwill** - Investment before request
3. **Match communication level** - Formal/casual, depth, emotional tone

### Applications
- **Games**: Free content unlocks willingness to engage (battle pass, cosmetics)
- **Marketing**: Free trials, educational content, samples create reciprocity
- **Therapy**: Therapist validation and support create therapeutic alliance

## Reactance

**What it is**: Psychological resistance to perceived threats to freedom/autonomy.

### Red Flags (trigger reactance)
- Heavy-handed persuasion
- Forced choices ("you must")
- Guilt-based messaging
- Ultimatums

### Avoiding Reactance
✅ Present multiple options
✅ Frame as invitation, not demand
✅ Respect autonomy explicitly
✅ Offer reason ("here's why this matters") vs. coercion

**Therapy example**: "Would you be open to trying this technique?" activates autonomy; "You need to do this" triggers reactance.

## Cognitive Load Theory

**Principle**: People have limited mental processing capacity; overload reduces comprehension and decision-making.

### Design Rules
- **Rule of 3**: Limit core message to 3 main points
- **Chunking**: Break complex info into smaller units
- **Progressive disclosure**: Reveal info in sequence, not all at once
- **Visual hierarchy**: Make important info visually prominent

### Applications
- **Games**: Tutorial pacing (don't dump all mechanics at once)
- **Marketing**: Headlines + sub-points structure (not walls of text)
- **Therapy**: Psychoeducation in digestible chunks

---

## Confirmation Bias

**What it is**: People seek, interpret, and remember information confirming existing beliefs.

### Strategic Applications
1. **Pre-existing beliefs alignment**: Lead with shared values
2. **Gradual belief shift**: Small shifts easier than dramatic reversals
3. **Peer confirmation**: Others with similar beliefs validate new information

### Caution
- Can be manipulative if used to reinforce false beliefs
- **Ethical use**: Provide full information; let audience decide
- **Therapy**: Help identify distorted thinking patterns

---

## Loss Aversion (Kahneman & Tversky)

**Principle**: Losses are psychologically ~2x more powerful than equivalent gains.

### Applications
- **Messaging framing**: "Save 50%" (loss avoidance) > "Get 50% more" (gain)
- **Games**: Fear of losing rewards motivates continuation
- **Therapy**: Reframe as preventing deterioration, not just gaining improvement
- **Marketing**: "Don't miss out" (loss frame) activates urgency

### Example
- ❌ "Join our program to feel better" (gain frame)
- ✅ "Without support, anxiety worsens; join to prevent escalation" (loss frame + agency)

---

## Reference Class Bias

**Issue**: People misjudge outcomes by focusing on specific case rather than statistical base rate.

### Correction
- Provide base rates explicitly: "Most people in your situation..."
- Use group data to calibrate expectations
- Normalize struggles: "This is a common challenge" (especially in therapy)

## Summary Matrix: When to Use Each Principle

| Principle | Games | Marketing | Therapy | Key Warning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|---------|------------|
| SDT | Autonomy in gameplay | Choice in purchase | Client agency | Never remove choice |
| Social Proof | Multiplayer metrics | Testimonials, reviews | Peer support | Must be authentic |
| Authority | NPCs, tutorials | Expert credentials | Therapist competence | Never fake it |
| Reciprocity | Free starter content | Free trials/samples | Therapeutic support | No strings invisible |
| Avoid Reactance | Open-ended quests | Opt-in marketing | Collaborative TX | Never force adoption |
| Cognitive Load | Tutorial pacing | Message structure | Psychoeducation | Test readability |
| Confirmation Bias | Narrative alignment | Values marketing | Identify distortions | Full transparency |
| Loss Aversion | Leveling stakes | Urgency in copy | Prevent deterioration | Don't exaggerate risk |

---

## Cross-Domain Principle

**Universal truth across all domains**: Trust precedes persuasion. Build trust first through transparency, competence, and respecting autonomy. Persuasion follows naturally.
