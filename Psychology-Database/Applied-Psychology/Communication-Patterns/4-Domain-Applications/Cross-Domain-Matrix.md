# Cross-Domain Communication Patterns Matrix

## Psychological Principle Comparison Across Domains

### Self-Determination Theory (SDT): Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness

| Domain | Autonomy Expression | Competence Expression | Relatedness Expression |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Games** | Choose class, quest order, playstyle | Progressive difficulty, skill trees, visible metrics | Guilds, multiplayer, shared community identity |
| **Marketing** | Opt-in communication, flexible plans, customization | Educational content, success metrics, onboarding | Brand community, shared values, belonging |
| **Therapy** | Collaborative goal-setting, pacing control, client voice | Skill mastery (coping), symptom improvement, progress metrics | Therapeutic relationship, group therapy, peer support, normalization |

**Universal principle**: All three needs must be addressed; missing one undermines engagement.

---

### Behavioral Triggers: When & How to Use

| Trigger | Games Application | Marketing Application | Therapy Application |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Scarcity** | Limited-time cosmetics, seasonal content | Flash sales, exclusive membership | Limited appointment slots, specialized programs |
| **Urgency** | Time-limited events, daily challenges | Deadline for offer, countdown | Session scheduling, treatment window |
| **Social Proof** | Leaderboards, player count, friend activity | Testimonials, reviews, "bestseller" | "Most people find this helpful," group therapy |
| **Authority** | NPC guides, expert NPCs | Expert credentials, media mentions | Therapist credentials, licensing, specialization |
| **Reciprocity** | Free starter content, daily bonuses | Free trial, educational content | Therapist attentiveness, initial validation |
| **Commitment** | Create character, join guild, set public goals | Newsletter signup, public wish-list | Written treatment plan, goal-setting |
| **Novelty** | New mechanics, story reveals, updates | New products, trend-based marketing | New techniques, psychoeducation, insights |
| **Autonomy** | Open-ended objectives, pace control | Choose plan type, customize experience | Choose approach, control pacing |
| **Competence Feedback** | XP bars, level-ups, visual feedback | Progress through funnel, metrics | Symptom improvement graphs, skill achievement |

**Meta-principle**: Authentic triggers (earned) beat manipulative ones. Transparency increases acceptability.

### Framing Effects: How to Reframe by Domain

| Frame Type | Games | Marketing | Therapy |
|-----------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Gain vs. Loss** | "Earn rewards" (approach) vs. "Don't lose streak" (avoidance) | "Save $50" (gain) vs. "Don't miss out" (loss) | "Build confidence" (gain) vs. "Prevent deterioration" (loss) |
| **Progress Measurement** | "Level 42/100" (progress) vs. "58 left to go" (remaining) | "Save 40%" (benefit) vs. "Regular price $150" (cost anchor) | "Improved 30%" (progress) vs. "Still 70% to go" (remaining) |
| **Causality** | "You made this choice" (agency) | "Experts recommend" (authority) vs. "You'll miss out" (external pressure) | "Your choices created this" + "You can change it" (balanced) |
| **Concrete vs. Abstract** | "Beat boss" (concrete) vs. "get stronger" (abstract) | "Saves $5000/year" (concrete) vs. "better life" (abstract) | "Practice this skill" (concrete) vs. "healing journey" (abstract) |

**Implementation**: Lead with concrete to activate behavior; abstract to justify meaning.

### Message Structure Patterns

| Structure | Games | Marketing | Therapy |
|-----------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Problem-Agitate-Solve** | "Losing lives → Frustrating → New strategy" | "Struggling with X → Emotional impact → Here's solution" | "This pattern developed → Painful consequences → Here's approach" |
| **Hook-Explanation-Action** | Tutorial hook → Teach mechanic → Practice | Relatable opening → Explain benefit → CTA | Validation → Psychoeducation → Homework |
| **Story Arc** | Hero's journey narrative | Customer transformation story | Client's journey toward wellbeing |

### Feedback & Motivation Timing

| Timescale | Games | Marketing | Therapy |
|-----------|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Immediate (< 1 sec)** | Hit feedback, sound effect | Page load confirmation | Therapist nod/validation during disclosure |
| **Short (seconds-minutes)** | Combat resolved, quest completed | Email confirmation, page acknowledgment | Within-session feedback ("That's a good insight") |
| **Medium (hours-days)** | Match summary, daily rewards | Email sequence, order shipment | Weekly progress check, symptom tracking |
| **Long (weeks-months)** | Level progression, seasonal rewards | Monthly engagement metrics, loyalty | Monthly outcome measurement, long-term progress |

**Principle**: Layered feedback sustains motivation across timescales.

### Engagement Lifecycle

#### Stage 1: ACTIVATION / AWARENESS
| Domain | Primary Goal | Key Message | Barrier to Address |
|--------|--|--|--|
| **Games** | Get player to download/start | "This game is for you" | Skepticism, time investment |
| **Marketing** | Get attention | "This solves your problem" | Crowded market, trust |
| **Therapy** | Get person to call | "Help is available; you're not alone" | Shame, fear, denial |

#### Stage 2: COMMITMENT / CONSIDERATION
| Domain | Primary Goal | Key Message | Barrier to Address |
|--------|--|--|--|
| **Games** | Reduce dropout in first hour | "You're doing great; keep going" | Difficulty, confusion, boredom |
| **Marketing** | Evaluate vs. competitors | "Here's why we're different" | Price, feature concerns, credibility |
| **Therapy** | Build therapeutic alliance | "I understand; we'll do this together" | Vulnerability, trust, hope |

#### Stage 3: ENGAGEMENT / DECISION
| Domain | Primary Goal | Key Message | Barrier to Address |
|--------|--|--|--|
| **Games** | Regular play sessions | "More content, challenges, community" | Boredom, skill ceiling, grind fatigue |
| **Marketing** | First purchase | "Here's how to get started" | Last-minute doubt, friction in checkout |
| **Therapy** | Consistent attendance and homework | "This is working; keep going" | Resistance, shame about progress, ruptures |

#### Stage 4: ADVOCACY / RETENTION
| Domain | Primary Goal | Key Message | Barrier to Address |
|--------|--|--|--|
| **Games** | User-created content, community leadership | "You're part of something" | Feeling like outsider, lack of recognition |
| **Marketing** | Repeat purchase, referral | "You helped others; thank you" | Complacency, competitive alternatives |
| **Therapy** | Skill transfer, independence | "You have what you need" | Fear of ending, loss of therapist, autonomy |

## Communication Anti-Patterns (Universal Across Domains)

### What Destroys Trust

| Anti-Pattern | Games Example | Marketing Example | Therapy Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hidden mechanics** | Loot drop rates not disclosed | Terms and conditions hidden | Therapy approach not explained |
| **Fabricated proof** | Fake player testimonials | Fake reviews or influencers | Outcomes exaggerated |
| **Surprise costs** | Hidden in-app purchases | Unexpected charges, auto-renewal | Billing surprises |
| **Unresponsive support** | Tickets ignored for weeks | Unresponded customer emails | Therapist unavailable for crises |
| **Bait and switch** | Advertise feature, remove it | Free trial becomes paid | Promise of specialization, deliver generic |
| **False urgency** | "Sale ends tonight" (repeating daily) | Countdown timer resets on refresh | "Must start treatment now" (arbitrary pressure) |
| **Shame messaging** | "Noobs" or elite-only language | Target insecurities exploitatively | Judgment or guilt-based approach |

**Universal principle**: **Transparency > Manipulation.** Short-term gains (clicks, sales, compliance) from manipulation cost long-term trust.

## Ethical Boundaries (Universal Framework)

### The Transparency Test

**Question**: "Would I feel good about this if the person fully knew my strategy?"

### By Domain

#### Games
- ✅ Cosmetics-only monetization (transparent power difference)
- ❌ Loot boxes with hidden drop rates (fabricated uncertainty)
- ✅ Seasonal content with known return (honest rarity)
- ❌ FOMO messaging with fake scarcity (manipulative)

#### Marketing
- ✅ Legitimate customer testimonials (authentic proof)
- ❌ Fake reviews (fraud)
- ✅ Actual limited-time offer (honest urgency)
- ❌ Sale that returns every week (false scarcity)
- ✅ Affiliate disclosure (transparency)
- ❌ Undisclosed paid promotion (deception)

#### Therapy
- ✅ Transparent treatment approach (informed consent)
- ❌ Hidden fees (financial deception)
- ✅ Clear confidentiality limits (ethical transparency)
- ❌ Dual relationships (boundary violation)
- ✅ Evidence-based treatment (research grounding)
- ❌ Promise of guaranteed cure (unrealistic expectation)

| **Enthusiasm** | High (celebrate wins, create excitement) | High (activate desire) | Moderate (hope + realism) |

| **Empathy** | Moderate (games don't require emotional processing) | Low-moderate (care about needs, not emotions) | High (emotional attunement critical) |

**Principle**: Match tone to context and audience; mismatch feels inauthentic.

## Crisis Communication Patterns

### When Something Goes Wrong

#### Games: Server Down / Game-Breaking Bug
- **Timing**: Immediate acknowledgment
- **Transparency**: "Here's what happened"
- **Action**: "Here's what we're doing"
- **Compensation**: Downtime reward
- **Prevention**: "Here's how we'll prevent this"

#### Marketing: Negative Review / Product Issue
- **Acknowledgment**: "We see the feedback"
- **Responsibility**: "We fell short"
- **Fix**: "Here's what we're doing"
- **Compensation**: "We'll make it right"
- **Prevention**: "Process improvements..."

#### Therapy: Client in Crisis / Therapist Unavailable
- **Safety**: "Let's ensure you're safe"
- **Alternative**: "Here are other resources"
- **Continuity**: "I'm back on [date]; until then..."
- **Action**: "If urgent, call crisis line"
- **Follow-up**: "Checking in when I return"

**Universal principle**: Early transparency + action > delay + excuses.

## Communication Frequency Patterns

### How Often to Communicate?

| Domain | Optimal Frequency | Danger Zone |
|--------|---|---|
| **Games** | Real-time feedback, daily check-ins, weekly events, monthly updates | Constant notifications → fatigue; silent > 1 month → disconnect |
| **Marketing** | 2-4x weekly email, 1-2x daily social, monthly deep content | >2x daily → unsubscribe; <1x weekly → forgotten |
| **Therapy** | 1x weekly session (primary), between-session homework, crisis availability | >1x weekly (codependency); <1x weekly (slow progress) |

**Principle**: Right frequency matches attention capacity; too much overwhelms; too little disengages.

## Summary: Unified Communication Framework

**Across all domains, effective communication requires**:

1. **Trust** (established via credibility, transparency, and follow-through)
2. **Clarity** (understood correctly; no jargon or confusion)
3. **Relevance** (for this specific person/audience right now)
4. **Autonomy respect** (choice and agency preserved)
5. **Appropriate pacing** (not too fast, not too slow)
6. **Consistent follow-up** (delivery on promises; reliability)
7. **Honest feedback** (earned, not fabricated)
8. **Psychological safety** (non-judgment, non-shame)
9. **Alignment** (what you say matches what you do)
10. **Cultural competence** (respecting diversity and context)

**The universal truth**: Manipulation works short-term; trust works long-term. Building sustainable engagement means serving the audience, not exploiting them.

## Quick Reference: When to Use Which Principle

| Goal | Principle | Caution |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **Activate action now** | Urgency + social proof + easy CTA | Don't use urgency constantly (habituates) |
| **Build sustained engagement** | Autonomy + competence + community | Don't sacrifice intrinsic for extrinsic |
| **Increase perceived value** | Scarcity + authority + social proof | Scarcity must be genuine (fabrication kills trust) |
| **Change behavior** | Clear incentive + feedback + small steps | Small frequent wins > large distant goals |
| **Build loyalty** | Community + meaning + reciprocity | Loyalty comes from consistency; quick wins don't last |
| **Overcome resistance** | Validation + autonomy + small asks | Respect autonomy; pushing triggers reactance |
| **Scale engagement** | Leverage communities + peer influence | Be careful with scale; personalization matters |

## Final Note

This matrix is a **framework**, not a cookbook. Psychology is about understanding human motivation and designing experiences that serve people. The goal is never manipulation for its own sake—it's creating genuine value, building trust, and helping people achieve meaningful change.

Use these principles ethically. Your long-term credibility and impact depend on it.
