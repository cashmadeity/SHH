# The 2025 APA Ethics Code: Expanded Principles & Forward-Looking Standards

**Status:** DRAFT (public comment closed March 2025; final version expected 2025-2026)

**Significance:** The first major revision to the APA Ethics Code since 2017. Expands from 5 to 8 principles to address modern challenges: technology, AI, climate, social justice.

**For app builders:** These principles are coming. Start implementing them now.

---

## New Principles (6-8): What's Changing

### Principle 6: Human & Civil Rights

**New in draft 2025**

**Intent:** Protect fundamental human rights, particularly for vulnerable populations.

**Key additions:**
- Explicit protection against discrimination
- Attention to systemic inequality
- Advocacy for human rights
- Protection for marginalized populations

**Application to psychology apps:**

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Don't build features that discriminate against vulnerable groups
- [ ] Acknowledge how discrimination affects mental health
- [ ] Support user rights even when costly
- [ ] Use tech to advance equality (not enable discrimination)

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Build features that harm vulnerable populations
- [ ] Ignore systemic barriers (homelessness, poverty, discrimination)
- [ ] Pretend technology is neutral (algorithms reflect biases)
- [ ] Profit from inequality

**Technical implications:**
- Audit AI for bias (especially for marginalized groups)
- Ensure accessibility (disability rights)
- Prevent re-identification of de-identified data (privacy as human right)
- Monitor for disparate impact (does app help some groups more than others?)

---

### Principle 7: Scientific Mindedness

**New in draft 2025**

**Intent:** Stay grounded in evidence; embrace uncertainty; evolve with science.

**Key additions:**
- Use evidence-based practices
- Acknowledge limitations & unknowns
- Update beliefs when evidence changes
- Avoid dogmatism

**Application to psychology apps:**

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Base features on current research (not outdated theories)
- [ ] Acknowledge what we don't know
- [ ] Update when new evidence emerges
- [ ] Test your app's effectiveness (don't just assume it works)
- [ ] Report negative findings (not just successes)

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Claim research support that doesn't exist
- [ ] Stick with outdated approaches because "that's what we've always done"
- [ ] Hide or ignore research that contradicts your approach
- [ ] Treat weak evidence as strong evidence
- [ ] Over-generalize findings beyond their scope

**Technical implications:**
- Continuous monitoring of research (subscribe to psychology journals)
- A/B testing to measure effectiveness
- Pre-registering studies (prevents cherry-picking results)
- Publishing methods transparently (others can replicate/verify)
- Updating features based on new evidence (even if it means admitting old feature didn't work)

---

### Principle 8: Environmental Stewardship

**New in draft 2025**

**Intent:** Consider impact on planetary wellbeing; recognize mental health as connected to environment.

**Key additions:**
- Environmental impact of practice/technology
- Climate anxiety as legitimate mental health issue
- Sustainability in service delivery
- Interconnectedness of human-environment wellbeing

**Application to psychology apps:**

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Minimize carbon footprint (energy-efficient servers, not blockchain gimmicks)
- [ ] Support user connection to nature (not replace it with app)
- [ ] Acknowledge climate/environmental factors in mental health
- [ ] Sustainable business model (not growth-at-all-costs)

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Ignore carbon cost of infrastructure
- [ ] Encourage unhealthy screen time over nature
- [ ] Dismiss climate anxiety as disorder to medicate
- [ ] Pretend individual wellness is separate from planetary health

**Technical implications:**
- Choose green cloud providers (renewable energy data centers)
- Efficient code (not bloated, battery-draining apps)
- Feature suggestions: time in nature, environmental action
- Content: acknowledge eco-anxiety as real response to real crisis
- Business: sustainable growth (not exponential extraction)

## Revised Core Principles (1-5): What's Changing

The 5 original principles remain, but with expanded language in draft 2025:

### Principle 1: Beneficence & Nonmaleficence (Revised)

**New emphasis:** Active prevention of harm, not just avoiding it.

**Changes:**
- ✅ (Old) Do no harm
- ➕ (New) Actively prevent harm; monitor for unintended consequences
- ➕ (New) Address disparities in harm (some groups harmed more)
- ➕ (New) Sustainability of benefit (help that lasts, not burnout)

**For apps:**
- Monitor for adverse effects (quarterly user surveys)
- Watch for disparate impact ("Does app help everyone equally?")
- Avoid short-term benefit with long-term harm (e.g., addiction mechanics)
- Plan for app sustainability (if you shut down, what happens to users?)

---

### Principle 2: Fidelity & Responsibility (Revised)

**New emphasis:** Responsibility extends to broader ecosystem, not just individual user.

**Changes:**
- ✅ (Old) Be trustworthy
- ➕ (New) Responsibility to profession (not just yourself)
- ➕ (New) Responsibility to society
- ➕ (New) Transparency about power dynamics

**For apps:**
- How does your app affect the mental health profession?
- Could your app undermine human therapists?
- Are you taking responsibility for societal impact?
- Are you transparent about power imbalances (app knows user; user doesn't know app)?

---

### Principle 3: Integrity (Revised)

**New emphasis:** Honesty in gray areas; intellectual humility.

**Changes:**
- ✅ (Old) Don't lie
- ➕ (New) Acknowledge uncertainty
- ➕ (New) Admit what you don't know
- ➕ (New) Transparency about conflicts of interest

**For apps:**
- Don't claim certainty you don't have
- Acknowledge limitations clearly (not in fine print)
- Disclose financial conflicts (funding, partnerships)
- Say "We don't know" when appropriate

---

### Principle 4: Justice (Revised)

**New emphasis:** Actively work against systemic injustice; go beyond individual fairness.

**Changes:**
- ✅ (Old) Treat people fairly
- ➕ (New) Address systemic inequality
- ➕ (New) Advocate for marginalized populations
- ➕ (New) Equity (not just equality—account for different starting points)

**For apps:**
- Testing with diverse populations isn't enough; commit to equity
- Use app to advance justice (not maintain status quo)
- Advocate: If your app shows depression rates higher in certain communities, speak up
- Equity: Free access for low-income users (not just "affordable")

---

### Principle 5: Respect (Refined)

**New emphasis:** Cultural humility; power dynamics.

**Changes:**
- ✅ (Old) Respect autonomy
- ➕ (New) Acknowledge power imbalance (therapist/app has power over client)
- ➕ (New) Cultural humility (not expertise)
- ➕ (New) Intersectionality (people have multiple identities that interact)

**For apps:**
- Recognize app has power over vulnerable user
- Don't exploit that power
- Learn from users about their culture (not teach them)
- Understand: A woman of color has different needs than white woman

## Draft 2025: New Standards (Not Just Principles)

The 2025 Code will include **10 sections of standards** (specific rules):

1. **Resolving Ethical Issues** — How to handle conflicts; complaint process
2. **Competence** — What you're qualified to do
3. **Human & Civil Rights** — Anti-discrimination standards (NEW)
4. **Privacy & Confidentiality** — Data protection
5. **Advertising & Public Statements** — Marketing standards; no false claims
6. **Record Keeping & Fees** — Financial transparency
7. **Education & Training** — Preparing future professionals
8. **Research & Publication** — Ethical research conduct
9. **Assessment** — Ethical testing practices
10. **Therapy & Counseling** — Relationship standards (expanded to include teletherapy/AI)

**For app builders:** Every standard applies to you. Read the final code when published (expected 2025-2026).

## Key Differences: 2017 vs. 2025 Draft

| Area | 2017 Code | 2025 Draft |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| **Scope** | 5 principles, 10 sections | 8 principles, 10 sections |
| **AI/Technology** | Minimal guidance | Dedicated section expected |
| **Systemic Issues** | Individual focus | Systemic/social justice focus |
| **Diversity** | Mentioned | Central theme |
| **Environment** | Not mentioned | New principle #8 |
| **Conflicts of interest** | General | More explicit |
| **Power dynamics** | Implicit | Explicit |
| **Remote/Digital** | Brief teletherapy note | Expanded section |

## Red Flags: 2025 Violations

### Flag 1: Ignoring Civil Rights

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Build app that discriminates (even unintentionally)
- [ ] Ignore disparities you observe
- [ ] Claim neutrality (all tech has bias)
- [ ] Refuse to fix discriminatory features

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Audit for discrimination proactively
- [ ] Fix disparities when found
- [ ] Acknowledge limitations transparently
- [ ] Advocate for your users' rights

### Flag 2: Dogmatism About Approach

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Claim "this is the only way"
- [ ] Ignore contradictory evidence
- [ ] Refuse to update based on research
- [ ] Treat outdated approaches as gospel

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Stay current with research
- [ ] Update when evidence changes
- [ ] Acknowledge competing approaches
- [ ] Evolve your app continuously

### Flag 3: Ignoring Environmental Impact

❌ **DON'T:**
- [ ] Design energy-hungry app (gaming servers model)
- [ ] Encourage screen addiction over nature
- [ ] Dismiss climate anxiety
- [ ] Build unsustainable business model

✅ **DO:**
- [ ] Use green infrastructure
- [ ] Support nature connection
- [ ] Address climate/environment in content
- [ ] Plan for long-term sustainability

## The Bigger Picture: Why These Changes?

The 2025 draft reflects where psychology is headed:

1. **Tech everywhere** → Need explicit digital ethics guidance
2. **Injustice + mental health intertwined** → Can't ignore systemic issues
3. **Climate crisis real** → Mental health inseparable from environmental health
4. **Science evolving fast** → Need flexibility, not dogmatism
5. **Power dynamics critical** → Apps wield tremendous influence

Apps built today with the 2017 code will feel outdated in 3 years. **Better to lead than follow.**
