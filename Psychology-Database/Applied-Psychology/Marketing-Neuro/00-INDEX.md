## 🧠 Quick Reference: Key Principles

| Concept | Definition | Application |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Neuromarketing** | Using neuroscience to decode subconscious consumer responses | Optimize ads, packaging, digital experiences |
| **Gamification** | Integrating game mechanics into non-game contexts | Increase engagement, motivation, adherence |
| **Behavioral Targeting** | Profiling consumers via psychological/neural data | Precision marketing, personalization |
| **Persuasion Trigger** | Stimuli activating reward centers/emotional responses | Colors, scarcity, social proof, movement |
| **Therapeutic Gaming** | Games designed for mental health outcomes | Anxiety treatment, PTSD, depression, ADHD |

## 🎮 Priority Research Areas

### 1. Video Games + Marketing
- Game mechanics that drive consumer behavior (progression, rewards, social proof)
- In-game advertising integration without breaking immersion
- Free-to-play monetization psychology
- Engagement loops & dopamine manipulation

### 2. Video Games + Therapy
- Games as FDA-approved treatments (e.g., EndeavorRx for ADHD)
- VR exposure therapy for anxiety/PTSD
- Skill-building through gamification (CBT, emotional regulation)
- Risk of over-engagement & gaming addiction

### 3. The Intersection: Ethical Boundaries
- Where marketing psychology crosses into therapeutic manipulation
- Protecting vulnerable populations (minors, mentally ill)
- Informed consent in behavioral targeting
- Balancing engagement vs. addiction risk

## 📊 Supported Research Methodologies

### Neuroimaging
- **fMRI** (blood flow imaging) → emotional engagement
- **EEG** (electrical activity) → attention, emotional intensity
- **MEG** (magnetic field) → high temporal resolution

### Physiological Tracking
- **Eye-tracking** → visual attention patterns
- **Facial coding** → micro-expressions, genuine emotion
- **GSR** (skin conductance) → arousal/stress levels
- **EMG** (muscle activity) → physiological responses

### Behavioral Assessment
- **Mobile tracking** → real-world behavior logging
- **A/B testing** → conversion optimization
- **Engagement metrics** → retention, dropout rates

---

## ⚠️ Ethical Guardrails

**Key findings from research:**
- ~95% of decisions are subconscious → high manipulation risk
- Vulnerable populations (children, mentally ill) require special protection
- Consent & transparency must be explicit, not implied
- Addiction risks in gamification require monitoring
- Long-term psychological impacts still under-researched
