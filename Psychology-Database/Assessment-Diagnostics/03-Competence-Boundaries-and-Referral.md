# Competence Boundaries & Referral Protocols

**Source:** APA Ethical Principles 2.04 (Competence), 3.03 (Conflicts with Organizational Demands), 9.07 (Assessment by Unqualified Persons)  
**Status:** Critical for preventing harm from over-scope  
**Scope:** Defining assessment scope, knowing your limits, when and how to refer

---

## Core Principle: Know What You Cannot Do

**APA Standard 2.04:**
> "Psychologists provide services, teach, and conduct research with populations and in areas only within the boundaries of their competence, based on their education, training, supervised experience, consultation, study, or professional experience."

Translation: **Just because you CAN measure something doesn't mean you SHOULD.**

---

## What is Competence in Assessment?

Competence = **knowledge + training + supervised experience + ongoing learning**

Not:
- ❌ Passing an online course
- ❌ Reading the test manual
- ❌ Having a psychology degree
- ❌ Working with a licensed supervisor

But:
- ✅ Formal training in assessment (graduate course or intensive workshop)
- ✅ Supervised practice (50+ cases under expert guidance)
- ✅ Knowledge of test theory & psychometrics
- ✅ Ongoing education & consultation

---

## Competence Tiers by Assessment Type

### Tier 1: Screening / Brief Psychometrics (Lower Barrier)

**Includes:** PHQ-9 (depression), GAD-7 (anxiety), AUDIT (alcohol), PCL-5 (PTSD)

**Minimum qualification:**
- Master's level psychology training, OR
- Supervised clinical experience, OR
- Licensed social worker/counselor

**Training needed:**
- Understand what test measures
- Know reliability & validity for YOUR population
- Know limitations (not diagnosis, false positive/negative rates)
- Can interpret results in context

**Supervision:**
- Formal training course (1-2 days to 1-2 weeks)
- Initial cases reviewed by expert
- Can become independent with practice (50+ cases)

**Red flags (you're NOT competent if):**
- [ ] You assume screening = diagnosis
- [ ] You can't explain the test's limitations
- [ ] You interpret identically for all people (ignoring context)
- [ ] You can't use group norms (percentiles, standard scores)
- [ ] You've never reviewed a case with feedback

### Tier 2: Cognitive / Ability Testing (Medium-High Barrier)

**Includes:** WAIS-IV (IQ), Woodcock-Johnson (achievement), WISC-V (children's cognitive)

**Minimum qualification:**
- **Licensed Psychologist (PhD/PsyD)** (some states: Master's with supervision)
- OR supervised trainee under licensed psychologist

**Training needed:**
- Graduate cognitive psychology course
- Psychometrics/measurement theory course
- Test-specific training (2-5 days)
- Supervised administration & interpretation (100+ cases)
- Knowledge of neuropsychology/development (context for scores)

**Supervision:**
- Every case reviewed by licensed psychologist initially
- Can become independent with explicit training + demonstrated competence

**Red flags (you're NOT competent if):**
- [ ] You can't explain standard scores, confidence intervals, percentiles
- [ ] You interpret score without considering educational/cultural background
- [ ] You don't know how to administer (precise timing, basal/ceiling rules)
- [ ] You can't rule out alternative explanations (depression, fatigue, language barrier)
- [ ] You've never taken a graduate psychometrics course

### Tier 3: Personality / Projective Testing (Highest Barrier)

**Includes:** MMPI-2 (clinical personality), Rorschach (projective), TAT (thematic apperception)

**Minimum qualification:**
- **Licensed Psychologist (PhD/PsyD)**
- Specialized training in this specific test

**Training needed:**
- Graduate assessment course (semester-long minimum)
- Advanced courses in personality/psychopathology
- Test-specific intensive training (1-2 weeks minimum)
- Supervised interpretation (200+ cases, often 5+ years)
- Consultation with expert throughout career

**Supervision:**
- All cases reviewed by licensed psychologist (some argue forever)
- Consultation required for complex cases

**Red flags (you're NOT competent if):**
- [ ] You learned on the job (not formal training)
- [ ] You interpret beyond what the test was designed for
- [ ] You can't explain why you scored it that way
- [ ] You disagree with research on the test's validity but use it anyway
- [ ] You refuse to acknowledge limitations (e.g., "Rorschach is 100% accurate")

### Tier 4: Forensic / High-Stakes Assessment (Highest Barrier + Ethical Constraints)

**Includes:** Fitness-for-duty, risk assessment for violence, disability evaluation, custody evaluation

**Minimum qualification:**
- **Licensed Psychologist (PhD/PsyD)**
- Specialized training in forensic assessment
- Direct supervision from forensic psychologist

**Additional ethical requirements:**
- Clear understanding of who the CLIENT is (yourself? referring lawyer? court?)
- Conflict-of-interest analysis
- Informed consent explicitly addressing forensic context
- Understanding of legal standards (state-specific)
- Immaculate documentation (legal review standard)

**Training needed:**
- Forensic psychology graduate course
- Test-specific training (often 2-5 days)
- Supervised cases (100+ under forensic expert)
- Continuing education (law changes frequently)
- Consultation on every case initially

**Red flags (you're NOT competent if):**
- [ ] You don't understand the legal context
- [ ] You haven't disclosed conflicts of interest
- [ ] You interpreted assessment to support a predetermined conclusion
- [ ] You don't know the difference between "reasonable doubt" and "beyond reasonable doubt"
- [ ] You're not documenting everything for legal review

```
ASSESSMENT TYPE | Licensed Psych? | Formal Training? | Supervision? | Competent? |
Depression screening | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
IQ testing | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
ADHD diagnosis | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
Custody evaluation | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
Risk assessment (violence) | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
Trauma/PTSD assessment | [ ] | [ ] | [ ] | YES/NO/CONDITIONAL |
```

**If you answer NO to any:**
- **Don't offer it** (ethical violation)
- **Refer out** (see referral section below)
- **Get training if you want to offer it** (formal training required, not self-taught)

**If you answer CONDITIONAL:**
- Clearly define the conditions (e.g., "Only with direct supervision of psychologist")
- Get written supervision agreement
- Document every case
- Plan exit strategy (what happens when you're unsupervised?)

## Red Flags: When to Refer

### Red Flag #1: Condition Beyond Your Training

**Examples:**
- Non-psychiatrist prescribing medication (beyond scope)
- Counselor doing forensic risk assessment without training
- Social worker diagnosing autism (requires psychoeducational testing + specialized training)

**Action:**
- Recognize immediately
- Refer to appropriate specialist
- Don't try to "figure it out" with the user
- Document referral

### Red Flag #2: Severity Beyond Your Setting

**Examples:**
- User reports active suicidal intent (you're a support app → refer to crisis line)
- Symptoms suggest psychosis (you're a depression app → refer to psychiatric evaluation)
- Trauma too severe for initial assessment (user needs stabilization first → refer to trauma specialist)

**Action:**
- Have crisis protocol (what % of users need crisis referral? expect high for severe populations)
- Provide warm handoff if possible (not just a link; actual referral)
- Document severity & referral path
- Follow up (did user get care?)

### Red Flag #3: Comorbidity You Can't Assess

**Examples:**
- User has depression + anxiety + "feeling disconnected from reality" (dissociation? psychosis? medication effect?)
- User has trauma symptoms + substance use (which caused which? affects treatment)
- User has depression + chronic pain (integrated assessment needed; you only assess depression)

**Action:**
- Refer for comprehensive assessment
- Don't patch together diagnoses in your lane
- Recognize your assessment's limits

### Red Flag #4: User Needs Different Modality Than You Offer

**Examples:**
- You offer app-based symptom tracking; user needs human interaction
- You assess depression; user needs couples therapy
- You screen for anxiety; user needs medication evaluation

**Action:**
- Recognize early
- Don't try to be everything
- Refer to appropriate provider

### Red Flag #5: Demographic/Cultural Mismatch

**Examples:**
- Assessment designed for Western, individualistic users; user is collectivist-culture
- Assessment normed on adults; user is elderly (different norms needed)
- Assessment in English; user is not fluent English speaker (language barrier)

**Action:**
- Refer to culturally-competent provider if available
- OR get cultural consultation before assessing
- OR adapt assessment (and validate the adaptation)
- Don't assume your assessment works universally

## Building Referral Protocols

### Step 1: Know Your Referral Network

Before you launch, map out:

```
REFERRAL NETWORK INVENTORY

CRISIS SERVICES:
[ ] National crisis line (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
[ ] Local emergency services (911)
[ ] Crisis text line (Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741)
[ ] Local psychiatric emergency room
[ ] Crisis stabilization unit (if available in your area)

ASSESSMENT SPECIALISTS:
[ ] Psychologist for IQ/cognitive testing
[ ] Psychiatrist for medication evaluation
[ ] ADHD specialist (neuropsychologist or developmental pediatrician for kids)
[ ] Trauma specialist (if you assess PTSD)
[ ] Substance abuse specialist (if you assess alcohol/drug use)
[ ] Cultural consultant (if you serve diverse populations)

ONGOING TREATMENT:
[ ] Therapist directory (Psychology Today, TherapyDen, etc.)
[ ] Psychiatry directory
[ ] Group therapy programs
[ ] Low-cost/sliding scale options (important!)
[ ] Telehealth providers (increasing access)

LEGAL/CUSTODY:
[ ] Forensic psychologist (if you refer custody cases)
[ ] Family law attorney (if needed)

YOUR OWN TEAM:
[ ] Clinical supervisor (if you're not independent)
[ ] Consultation psychologist (for hard cases)
[ ] Ethics consultant (if ethical question)
```

### Step 2: Create Referral Criteria

Document: **When do I refer, and to whom?**

```
REFERRAL CRITERIA MATRIX

Symptom/Issue | Severity | Your Action | Refer To |
Depression | Mild (PHQ-9: 5-9) | Provide resources, track | Self-help resources |
" | Moderate (10-19) | Encourage therapy, medication eval | Therapist or psychiatrist |
" | Moderate-Severe (20-27) | Strong recommendation | Therapist + psychiatrist |
" | Severe (28+) + suicidal | CRISIS PROTOCOL | 988 or ER |
" | Severe + psychotic features | CRISIS PROTOCOL | Psychiatric ER |
Anxiety | Mild | Provide resources | Self-help |
" | Moderate-Severe | Therapist evaluation | CBT-trained therapist |
" | Severe + panic | Medication evaluation | Psychiatrist |
Substance Use | Screening positive | Refer for assessment | Addiction specialist |
" | Active use + other diagnosis | Refer (integrated treatment) | Addictions psychiatrist |
Trauma/PTSD | High PTSD symptoms | Refer for assessment | Trauma specialist |
" | Active flashbacks/dissociation | May need stabilization first | Crisis services + trauma specialist |
Suicidal | Any intent or plan | IMMEDIATE crisis protocol | 988 or ER |
Homicidal | Any intent | IMMEDIATE crisis protocol | Law enforcement + ER |
```

### Step 3: Make Referral Warm, Not Cold

**Cold referral (bad):**
- App shows: "You should see a therapist."
- User: Okay... [never follows up]

**Warm referral (good):**
- App shows: "Based on your answers, I think talking to a professional would help. Here are therapists in your area, with:
  - Location/telehealth options
  - Insurance accepted
  - Specialty (trauma-informed, CBT, etc.)
  - Cost estimate
  - Link to book appointment
  - Option: Email therapist profile to yourself"

### Step 4: Document the Referral

When you refer, record:

```
USER REFERRAL LOG

Date: [when referred]
User ID: [de-identified]
Reason for referral: [e.g., "Suicidal ideation"]
Severity: [mild/moderate/severe]
Referred to: [999 crisis line / Dr. Smith / etc.]
Method: [in-app resource / text link / warm handoff]
User acknowledgment: [did they confirm receipt?]
Follow-up: [did we check if they got help?]

Outcome (optional, if followable):
- [ ] User called crisis line (self-reported or inferred from follow-up)
- [ ] User booked therapy appointment
- [ ] User didn't follow up (no further info)
- [ ] User reported getting help elsewhere
```

### Step 5: Crisis Protocol

Special case: **Imminent harm to self or others**

```
CRISIS PROTOCOL

AUTOMATIC TRIGGER:
- User reports active suicidal intent/plan
- User reports active homicidal intent/plan
- User reports active psychosis affecting judgment
- User reports severe dissociation/self-harm urge

IMMEDIATE ACTION:
1. Do NOT delay
2. Show crisis line number + direct call button (not link)
   "Call 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (FREE, confidential, 24/7)"
   [CALL NOW BUTTON]

3. In parallel, consider:
   - Calling 911 if immediate danger (if you have user contact info)
   - Contacting emergency contact (if user provided)
   - Can't do 911? Encourage user to call

DOCUMENTATION:
- Time of report
- What user said (verbatim if possible)
- What you did (what resource offered)
- Whether user acknowledged
- ANY follow-up

AFTER CRISIS:
- Do NOT restart assessment same day
- Encourage user to reach out after stabilization
- Follow up in 48-72 hours (if contact info available)
- Document outcome if known

LIMITATION DISCLOSURE:
- Apps cannot call 911 (yet)
- If you can't reach user: Document the limit
- Be transparent: "This is not emergency monitoring"
```

## Training Your Team

### If You're Hiring an Assessor

**Verify competence:**

```
ASSESSOR QUALIFICATION CHECKLIST

EDUCATION:
[ ] Degree in psychology (BA minimum, MA/PhD preferred)
[ ] Assessment-specific training (not just general training)
[ ] Coursework in psychometrics/measurement theory
[ ] Documentation: Transcripts, course descriptions

SUPERVISED EXPERIENCE:
[ ] Minimum # of supervised cases (depends on assessment type; see Tiers above)
[ ] Supervision by licensed professional (not peer)
[ ] Documentation: Supervision logs, case reviews
[ ] Duration: Typically 6-12 months minimum

ONGOING EDUCATION:
[ ] Commitment to continuing education (ethics requirement)
[ ] Evidence: Workshop attendance, readings, consultation
[ ] Annual plan for maintaining competence

EMOTIONAL/INTERPERSONAL COMPETENCE:
[ ] Can tolerate uncertainty (doesn't over-interpret)
[ ] Seeks consultation when unsure
[ ] Receives critical feedback without defensiveness
[ ] Respects user autonomy (doesn't diagnose & dismiss)
[ ] Can explain limitations clearly

ASSESSMENT-SPECIFIC KNOWLEDGE:
[ ] Can explain what test measures
[ ] Knows reliability, validity, sensitivity, specificity
[ ] Knows population norms (if different from user)
[ ] Can identify when to refer
[ ] Can handle unexpected/negative results appropriately
```

### Ongoing Supervision

If your assessor is a trainee or new hire:

```
SUPERVISION SCHEDULE

First 50 cases:
- 100% case review before sharing results with user
- Weekly 1-on-1 supervision (1 hour minimum)
- Group supervision (if available)

Cases 51-100:
- 50% case review (every other case, or all "hard" cases)
- Bi-weekly supervision
- Assessor presents cases to group

Cases 100+:
- 10-20% review (random sample, or all concerning cases)
- Monthly supervision or as-needed
- Assessor consults on difficult cases

SUPERVISION CONTENT:
- Technical (scoring accuracy, interpretation logic)
- Ethical (informed consent, referral, boundaries)
- Interpersonal (how to communicate results to user)
- Diversity (cultural adaptation, implicit bias)
- Self-reflection (assessor's own biases/limitations)

DOCUMENTATION:
- Supervision date, content, cases reviewed
- Issues identified & action plans
- Assessor competence signed off quarterly
```

## Common Competence Violations (What NOT to Do)

### Violation #1: Scope Creep

**Example:** "I'm trained in depression screening. Let me also assess anxiety, ADHD, trauma, and personality."

**Problem:** Different assessments require different training. You're not automatically competent in all of them.

**Fix:** Get specific training for each assessment type before offering it.

### Violation #2: Unsupervised Expansion

**Example:** You were trained to assess depression with supervision. Now you're unsupervised, so you expand to assess anxiety/ADHD without additional training.

**Problem:** Supervision was part of your competence. Expanding without training = incompetence.

**Fix:** Either stay in your lane or get training + new supervision plan.

### Violation #3: Outdated Knowledge

**Example:** You learned assessment 10 years ago. You're still using old norms, old interpretations, ignoring new research.

**Problem:** Assessment field evolves. Outdated knowledge = incompetence.

**Fix:** Annual continuing education, subscribe to updates on your assessments, attend workshops.

### Violation #4: Refusing to Refer

**Example:** User has possible bipolar disorder (beyond depression screening scope). You: "It's probably just depression. Try this intervention."

**Problem:** You're practicing outside your scope, preventing user from getting appropriate care.

**Fix:** Recognize it immediately, refer.

### Violation #5: Beyond Your Setting

**Example:** You're a support app. User reports active suicidal intent. You: "Let's work through this together in the app."

**Problem:** Your setting (digital, asynchronous) is inadequate for crisis. User needs human intervention.

**Fix:** Crisis protocol → immediate crisis services referral.

## Communicating Boundaries to Users

### Be Transparent

```
EXAMPLE: GOOD TRANSPARENCY

What This App Can Do:
✅ Screen for depression symptoms
✅ Help you track mood over time
✅ Provide educational resources
✅ Suggest when to talk to a doctor

What This App Cannot Do:
❌ Diagnose depression (only a doctor can)
❌ Replace therapy or medication
❌ Handle emergencies (call 988 for crisis)
❌ Provide personalized treatment plan (needs professional)
❌ Work for severe depression without professional help

When to See a Doctor:
- You're having thoughts of harming yourself
- Your symptoms are getting worse despite treatment
- You're having trouble with daily activities
- You've been depressed for more than 2 weeks
- You feel hopeless or lose interest in things you love

We'll tell you when to seek help. If we think you need professional care, 
we'll provide resources and encourage you to reach out.
```

### What NOT to Say

❌ "This app can diagnose depression"  
❌ "Our algorithm is 100% accurate"  
❌ "You don't need to see a doctor; we can help"  
❌ "We monitor you 24/7" (if you don't)  
❌ "This is approved by the FDA" (unless it is)

## Summary: Competence Checklist

### Before You Launch Any Assessment App

```
SCOPE DEFINITION
[ ] Clearly define what you assess (depression? anxiety? ADHD?)
[ ] Clearly define what you DON'T assess
[ ] Documented in your app, website, informed consent

TEAM COMPETENCE
[ ] Each assessor has documented training for what they do
[ ] Each assessor has supervised experience (not just training)
[ ] Supervision plan in place (who reviews cases? how often?)
[ ] Continuous learning plan (annual education documented)

REFERRAL NETWORK
[ ] Crisis referral pathway established
[ ] Specialty referral network built (psych, ADHD specialist, etc.)
[ ] Warm handoff process designed (not just links)
[ ] Referral criteria documented & followed

BOUNDARIES
[ ] Clear communication: What we can/can't do
[ ] Recognizable red flags (your team knows when to refer)
[ ] Escalation protocol (what if something is beyond our scope?)
[ ] Documentation of every referral

MONITORING
[ ] Regular case review (supervision)
[ ] Competence assessment (annual)
[ ] Incident tracking (when we miss something)
[ ] Continuous improvement based on incidents
```
