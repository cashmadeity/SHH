### How Communication Patterns Applies Each Ethical Principle

#### 1. Beneficence (Do Good)
- **Document reference:** All documents
- **Key sections:**
  - [1: Therapeutic-Tone] Section 4 (Grounding) & Section 5 (Tone)
  - [2: Active-Listening] Core principle
  - [3: Crisis] Immediate escalation
  - [6: Red-Flags] Category 1 (Over-promising harms)
- **Bottom line:** Communication that centers wellbeing over engagement = beneficence

#### 2. Non-Maleficence (Avoid Harm)
- **Document reference:** Especially 3, 5, 6
- **Key sections:**
  - [3: Crisis] All (recognizing harm)
  - [5: Technology-Ethics] Categories about false intimacy, over-reliance
  - [6: Red-Flags] All categories (each describes a harm)
- **Bottom line:** Know what communication patterns cause harm

#### 3. Fidelity (Build Trust)
- **Document reference:** Especially 1, 2, 5
- **Key sections:**
  - [1: Therapeutic-Tone] Section 5 (Boundaried but warm)
  - [2: Active-Listening] Pillar 1 (Full attention)
  - [5: Technology-Ethics] "Clarify relationship" section
  - [6: Red-Flags] Category 3 (Boundary violations)
- **Bottom line:** Consistency, honesty, presence = trust

#### 4. Integrity (Be Honest)
- **Document reference:** Especially 1, 5, 6
- **Key sections:**
  - [1: Therapeutic-Tone] Section 4 (Grounding in reality)
  - [5: Technology-Ethics] All of AI section
  - [6: Red-Flags] Category 1 (Over-promising) & 5 (Hiding info)
- **Bottom line:** Don't exaggerate, don't hide, stay honest about limits

#### 5. Justice (Ensure Equity)
- **Document reference:** Especially 4, 6
- **Key sections:**
  - [4: Cultural-Competence] All (especially audit section)
  - [6: Red-Flags] Category 9 (Failing to accommodate difference)
  - [Ethics-Guardrails/Best-Practices/Cultural-Competence.md](../Ethics-Guardrails/Best-Practices/Cultural-Competence.md)
- **Bottom line:** Language that works for your demographic works for some; audit across all demographics

#### 6. Respect for Dignity & Autonomy
- **Document reference:** All documents, especially 1, 2, 4
- **Key sections:**
  - [1: Therapeutic-Tone] Core principle (validate, empower)
  - [2: Active-Listening] Pillar 5 (Reflection respects person's reality)
  - [4: Cultural-Competence] Section 3 (Cultural humility)
  - [6: Red-Flags] Category 6 (Dependency) & 8 (Coercive language)
- **Bottom line:** Center person's voice, agency, and values

## Glossary of Key Terms

### Therapeutic Terms
- **Therapeutic alliance:** Collaborative relationship between helper and person; strongest predictor of outcome
- **Validation:** Acknowledging someone's experience as real and understandable
- **De-pathologizing:** Treating normal human experience as normal, not disordered
- **Reflection:** Mirroring back what you hear to show understanding and invite clarification
- **Empathy vs. Sympathy:** Empathy = understanding their experience; Sympathy = feeling sorry for them

### Ethics Terms
- **Beneficence:** Doing good
- **Non-maleficence:** Avoiding harm
- **Fidelity:** Building trust and being accountable
- **Integrity:** Honesty and accuracy
- **Justice:** Equitable access and quality
- **Respect:** Honoring autonomy, privacy, dignity

### Communication Terms
- **Synchronous:** Real-time communication (call, video, in-person)
- **Asynchronous:** Delayed communication (text, email, app check-in)
- **False intimacy:** Sense of closeness that isn't authentic (common with AI)
- **Scope creep:** Trying to do too much; overextending expertise
- **Cultural humility:** Ongoing self-reflection and learning about culture (vs. mastering it)
