# Communication Patterns & Therapeutic Language Knowledge Base

**A comprehensive guide for ethical, effective therapeutic communication in psychology applications.**

---

## Overview

This knowledge base provides concrete frameworks, examples, scripts, and decision trees for communicating therapeutically. It's designed for anyone building mental health technology or training to provide psychological support.

**Built on:** APA Ethical Principles, therapeutic alliance research, crisis intervention standards, and real-world patterns from practice.

**Scope:** How to *communicate* in ways that heal, not harm.

### 6. **Red Flags in Language** (21 KB)
Dangerous communication patterns that harm users while appearing therapeutic.
- Category 1: Over-promising & false hope (cure, guarantees, research misrepresentation, medication claims)
- Category 2: Emotional manipulation (guilt, FOMO, manufactured urgency, exploitation of loneliness)
- Category 3: Boundary violations (false credentials, inappropriate self-disclosure, scope creep)
- Category 4: Pathologizing language (labeling normal as disordered, shame-based language)
- Category 5: Withholding important information (hiding limitations, hiding risks)
- Category 6: Creating unhealthy dependency (over-engagement mechanics, artificial scarcity)
- Category 7: Language that prevents real help (discouraging professional care, treating serious issues as routine)
- Category 8: Dismissing real concerns (gaslighting, tone-deaf responses)
- Category 9: Coercive language ("must," "should")
- Category 10: Failing to accommodate difference (one-model-fits-all)
- Quick reference table
- Red flag detector checklist
- Red flag intensity scale

**When to use:** Auditing copy before launch, training writers, ethics review, identifying harmful patterns

## Key Principles (Across All Documents)

### 1. **Therapeutic Alliance is Everything**
Research shows: Quality of relationship predicts outcomes more than specific technique.
→ Our communication must build and maintain that alliance.

### 2. **Center the Person's Voice**
Not the app's voice. Not the therapist's agenda. Not the algorithm's output.
→ Everything we write should serve their agency and wisdom.

### 3. **Honesty Over Marketing**
Don't exaggerate. Don't hide. Don't promise what you can't deliver.
→ Trust is fragile. Maintain it ruthlessly.

### 4. **Respect Difference**
Culture, identity, spirituality, disability, neurodivergence—all of it shapes mental health.
→ One-size-fits-all is harmful.

### 5. **Real Help Beats Convenience**
An app that keeps someone in the app is failing. An app that supports them toward real people is succeeding.
→ We're tools. Not replacements.

## Statistics & Research

**What we know:**
- Therapeutic alliance predicts outcome better than technique (Horvath & Symonds, 1991; Baldwin et al., 2007)
- Active listening & empathy are core healing factors (Barrett-Lennard, 1962)
- Validation before problem-solving increases openness (Carl Rogers, person-centered therapy)
- Reflection helps people hear themselves and gain clarity (motivational interviewing research)
- Telehealth outcomes ≈ in-person for most conditions (but require intentional design)
- AI can support but not replace human connection (Torous et al., 2020+)

**What we don't know:**
- Long-term outcomes of app-only therapy
- Whether digital-first care widens or narrows equity gaps
- How AI affects therapeutic relationship over years
- Generalization across cultures (most research is Western samples)

**Implication:** Be humble. This KB reflects best practices as we know them. New research may shift guidance. Stay current.
