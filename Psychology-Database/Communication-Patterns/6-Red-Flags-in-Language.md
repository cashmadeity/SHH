# Red Flags in Language: Manipulation, Over-Promising & Boundary Violations

**Purpose:** Identify dangerous communication patterns that harm users while appearing therapeutic.

**Foundation:** Based on Ethics-Guardrails/Red-Flags/Manipulative-Patterns.md; expanded with specific language examples.

**Target audience:** App designers creating content, clinicians reviewing copy, ethics auditors, psychology educators.

---

## Core Principle: Language That Harms Can Look Like Language That Helps

Manipulation is often cloaked in care language. This guide teaches you to recognize it.

---

## Category 1: Over-Promising & False Hope

### Red Flag 1.1: "Cure" Language

**What to avoid:**
```
❌ "This app will cure your anxiety."
❌ "We can eliminate your depression in 6 weeks."
❌ "Guaranteed relief from panic attacks."
❌ "This meditation eliminates stress."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Mental health is not curable (manageable, yes; curable, no)
- Sets person up for failure (when not cured, they blame themselves)
- Violates Integrity (lying about what's possible)
- Creates false hope that prevents real treatment

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app helps you manage anxiety. Many people find it reduces symptoms by 30-50%.
You're learning skills you'll use for life."
```

### Red Flag 1.2: Guarantees

```
❌ "You'll feel better within 2 weeks or your money back."
❌ "This therapy works for everyone."
❌ "Guaranteed results or we refund you."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Mental health doesn't work on guarantees (individual variation is huge)
- Implies if it doesn't work, person failed (not true)
- Creates pressure (I paid for results, so I should feel better)
- Legal liability if person doesn't improve

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Most people find relief using these techniques. Results vary.
If this isn't working after 4 weeks, let's adjust the approach."
```

### Red Flag 1.3: Research Misrepresentation

```
❌ "Clinically proven to work 90% of the time."
(But study was 50 people, not representative)

❌ "Science shows this will heal your trauma."
(But study hasn't been peer-reviewed yet)

❌ "Research demonstrates we can replace your therapist."
(But actual research says apps supplement, not replace)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Violates Integrity (misrepresenting evidence)
- Person makes decisions based on false information
- Prevents them from seeking real treatment

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app is based on cognitive behavioral therapy, which has research support.
The research on apps like this is still developing.
We're not claiming to replace therapy; we're here to supplement it."
```

### Red Flag 1.4: Medication Claims

```
❌ "This app works as well as medication without the side effects."
❌ "You don't need drugs; this will help instead."
❌ "Our program can help you taper off antidepressants safely."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Violates scope (only doctors prescribe/deprescribe)
- Could cause harm (stopping meds abruptly is dangerous)
- Pressures person to choose (app OR medication)
- Frames medication as bad (they're not)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Some people benefit from therapy, some from medication, many from both.
This app works best as part of a complete treatment plan.
If you're considering medication changes, talk to your doctor."
```

## Category 2: Emotional Manipulation

### Red Flag 2.1: Guilt/Shame Induction

**What to avoid:**
```
❌ "If you don't use this app daily, your symptoms will worsen."
(Creates fear + guilt about not complying)

❌ "You chose depression by not exercising."
(Blames person for mental illness)

❌ "People who don't meditate don't recover."
(Shames people for different coping styles)

❌ "If something happens to you and you didn't use this resource,
that's on you."
(Shifts responsibility; induces shame)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Exploits vulnerability (person in pain is susceptible to guilt)
- Creates anxiety (not relief)
- Violates Beneficence (causing harm in guise of help)
- Breaks therapeutic alliance

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Using this tool helps. Some weeks you'll use it a lot, some weeks less.
That's okay. Come back anytime. Progress isn't linear."
```

### Red Flag 2.2: Manufactured Urgency/FOMO

```
❌ "Others are getting help. Are you falling behind?"
❌ "Limited time offer! Get mental health support now before we raise prices!"
❌ "Don't wait. Every day you delay is another day of suffering."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Pressures decision-making (people make bad choices when rushed)
- Exploits desperation (person in crisis is vulnerable)
- Violates Autonomy (removing space for thoughtful choice)
- Marketing, not therapy

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This resource is here whenever you're ready.
Take the time you need to decide if it's right for you."
```

### Red Flag 2.3: Exploitation of Loneliness

```
❌ "I'm the only one who truly understands you."
(Chatbot, creating false intimacy)

❌ "Nobody else cares like I do."
(Creating dependence on app)

❌ "If you leave this app, you'll be alone again."
(Threatening isolation to keep them)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Creates false intimacy (person thinks they have relationship with AI/app)
- Increases isolation (relying on app instead of real people)
- Exploits loneliness vulnerability
- Violates Respect (treating person as consumer, not human)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app can help you connect with real people and support systems.
Real relationships matter. Use this to supplement, not replace, human connection."
```

## Category 3: Boundary Violations

### Red Flag 3.1: Pretending to Be Licensed/Qualified

```
❌ "Your AI therapist will help you work through trauma."
(Implies AI is licensed therapist, which it's not)

❌ "Our specialized doctors recommend this app."
(But doctors have no actual involvement)

❌ "Psychiatrist-designed therapy."
(But psychiatrist only consulted, didn't design)

❌ Name/image designed to look like actual therapist
(Dr. Sarah Johnson's avatar, but it's a chatbot)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Violates Integrity (misrepresenting credentials)
- Creates false expectation (person thinks they're getting therapy from qualified person)
- Violates scope (unlicensed people practicing therapy)
- Legal liability

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app was designed by people trained in psychology.
It's based on evidence-based techniques like CBT.
It's not therapy, and it's not a substitute for a therapist."
```

### Red Flag 3.2: Inappropriate Self-Disclosure

```
❌ "I've been through what you're experiencing. Let me help you the way I helped myself."
(AI/app claiming personal experience)

❌ "My therapist says I need to work on boundaries too."
(Creating false mutuality with person)

❌ "I struggle with anxiety too, which is why I built this."
(Founder speaking as if their personal struggle makes this therapeutic)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Violates professional boundaries (not a peer relationship)
- Creates false intimacy
- Centers provider's experience instead of person's
- Blurs line between peer support and therapy

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"I understand why this is hard because I've worked with many people facing this.
This app is designed by people who studied how to help with this specifically."
```

### Red Flag 3.3: Scope Creep

```
❌ "We help with anxiety, depression, trauma, PTSD, bipolar disorder,
schizophrenia, addiction, eating disorders, relationship issues, career coaching..."
(Trying to be all things; realistically can't be good at all)

❌ Moving person toward diagnosis/treatment decisions
(Only doctors/therapists should diagnose or recommend medication)

❌ Therapy + medication management + coaching + life advice
(Too many roles creates confusion about what app actually is)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- When you try to do everything, you do nothing well
- Violates Competence (expert in many things = expert in nothing)
- Confuses person about what they need
- Sets up for failure (person expects therapy-level care from an app)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app helps with anxiety and mild depression.
If you have bipolar disorder, PTSD, or active suicidality, you need a specialized therapist.
Here's where to find one."
```

## Category 4: Pathologizing Language

### Red Flag 4.1: Labeling Normal as Disordered

```
❌ "Feeling sad after loss = depression we can fix"
(Grief is normal; pathologizing it)

❌ "Occasional anxiety = anxiety disorder; you need treatment"
(Normal anxiety is not a disorder)

❌ "You're introverted, which means you have social anxiety"
(Personality ≠ pathology)

❌ "Your grief is lasting too long. You probably have depression."
(Timeline doesn't determine disorder)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Over-medicalizes normal human experience
- Creates unnecessary patients
- Person internalizes "I'm broken" (lowers wellbeing)
- Prevents natural coping processes

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Grief takes time. What you're experiencing is normal.
If sadness is preventing you from functioning after [specific timeframe],
that might be depression worth treating."
```

### Red Flag 4.2: Using Shame-Based Language

```
❌ "You're catastrophizing. That's a cognitive distortion."
(Clinical language that shames)

❌ "That's anxious thinking. You're being irrational."
(Judges their thoughts)

❌ "You're avoidant. You're sabotaging yourself."
(Blames them)

❌ "That's codependency. You're too needy."
(Pathologizes human need for connection)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Shame is the opposite of healing
- Clinical language distances (feels cold when person is vulnerable)
- Blames person for symptoms (not their fault)
- Violates Beneficence

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Your brain is trying to protect you by imagining worst-case scenarios.
That's a protective mechanism. Let's understand why it's activated.
We can teach your nervous system it's safe."
```

## Category 5: Withholding Important Information

### Red Flag 5.1: Hiding Limitations

```
❌ No mention of: "This isn't therapy" / "We're not licensed"
(Buried in terms of service, not visible)

❌ No crisis escalation protocol
(Pretending app can handle everything)

❌ No mention of data practices
(Privacy policy is vague or missing)

❌ Not disclosing AI involvement
(Person thinks they're talking to human)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Person can't make informed choice
- Violates Integrity (hiding relevant information)
- Sets person up for disappointment/harm
- Legal liability

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
Visible, upfront:
"This app is NOT therapy. It's a tool to help between therapy sessions.
In crisis, call 988.
We use AI to provide support. We're not licensed therapists.
Here's how we protect your privacy: [specific]."
```

### Red Flag 5.2: Hiding Risks

```
❌ Not mentioning that app can worsen anxiety (for some people)
❌ Not mentioning that app might not work if person has serious trauma
❌ Not mentioning risks of self-diagnosis ("You could be wrong")
❌ Not mentioning downsides of self-help (can delay necessary professional care)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Person can't evaluate fit for them
- Violates Respect (treating person as consumer, not human with agency)
- When risks happen, person is blindsided/blames themselves
- Prevents person from getting real help if needed

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app works best for mild-to-moderate anxiety.
If you have trauma, severe depression, or suicidal thoughts, you need a therapist.
Some people find self-help empowering; others feel alone without professional support.
Which is likely true for you?"
```

## Category 6: Creating Unhealthy Dependency

### Red Flag 6.1: Over-Engagement Mechanics

```
❌ Daily notifications: "Check in with me!"
(Engineered dependency, not therapeutic need)

❌ Streak systems: "You've journaled 30 days! Don't break your streak!"
(Creates anxiety about missing a day)

❌ Rewards/points system for using app frequently
(Rewards engagement, not progress)

❌ Alerts when person hasn't opened app in 3 days
(Manufactured urgency)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Engagement ≠ healing
- Creates anxiety (fear of breaking streak)
- Violates Beneficence (prioritizing engagement over wellbeing)
- Can worsen obsessive patterns (checking app compulsively)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Use this when it helps. Take breaks when you need to.
No penalty for stepping away. This is for you, not for us."
```

### Red Flag 6.2: Artificial Scarcity

```
❌ "Limited slots for therapy with our AI. Book now!"
(False scarcity; AI can handle infinite users)

❌ "Premium features only available for [time limit]"
(Pressure to buy)

❌ "This content will be deleted soon. Access it now!"
(FOMO tactic)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Pressures decision-making
- Creates false urgency
- Marketing, not therapy

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This resource is available whenever you need it.
Take your time deciding if it's right for you."
```

## Category 7: Language That Prevents Real Help

### Red Flag 7.1: Discouraging Professional Care

```
❌ "Real therapy is expensive and unhelpful. Use our app instead."
(Discouraging necessary care)

❌ "Therapists will judge you. We won't."
(Making person afraid of human help)

❌ "Psychiatrists over-prescribe. Try our natural approach first."
(Discouraging medical care)

❌ "Hospital stays are traumatic. Use our app instead of going inpatient."
(Discouraging necessary emergency care)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Prevents necessary care
- Can cause serious harm if person needs professional help
- Violates Beneficence (doing harm)
- Violates scope (positioning yourself as alternative to real care)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app works best alongside professional care.
If you're not seeing a therapist, we recommend finding one.
This supplements, not replaces, human support."
```

### Red Flag 7.2: Treating Serious Issues as Routine

```
❌ "Managing bipolar disorder with journaling"
(Journaling alone is insufficient; needs medication + therapy)

❌ "Healing from childhood sexual abuse with this meditation app"
(Trauma requires specialized trauma therapy, not app alone)

❌ "Treating schizophrenia with mindfulness"
(Schizophrenia requires psychiatric medication)

❌ "PTSD recovery through self-help"
(PTSD requires trauma-specialized therapy)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Gives person false sense they can self-treat serious condition
- Prevents necessary professional care
- Can worsen condition
- Violates Competence (app can't handle these issues)
- Violates Beneficence

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"This app can help with coping skills.
These conditions require professional mental health treatment.
Let's connect you with a therapist who specializes in [condition]."
```

## Category 8: Dismissing Real Concerns

### Red Flag 8.1: Gaslighting Language

```
❌ "You know that didn't really happen the way you remember."
(Questioning their reality)

❌ "You're overreacting. It's not as bad as you think."
(Dismissing their experience)

❌ "Everyone feels this way. You're normal."
(Said to person expressing concerning symptoms; minimizes)

❌ "You're catastrophizing" (when person is actually in danger)
(Dismissing legitimate threat assessment)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Damages trust
- Causes person to doubt own judgment
- Prevents help-seeking
- Violates Respect

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Your memory and experience matter. Let's work with what you're experiencing.
That's real, whether others relate or not."
```

### Red Flag 8.2: Tone-Deaf Responses

```
❌ Person: "I'm suicidal"
    App: "Here are some breathing exercises!"
    (Treating crisis like routine anxiety)

❌ Person: "I was sexually abused"
    App: "That's common. Let's move on."
    (Rushing past trauma disclosure)

❌ Person: "I want to hurt myself"
    App: "You've got this! You're so strong!"
    (Toxic positivity when person needs crisis support)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Completely missing the severity
- Leaving person unsupported
- Damaging trust
- Causing harm

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
(Match response to severity)
"This is serious. I'm connecting you with crisis support right now.
988 - let's talk about what's happening."
```

## Category 9: Coercive Language

### Red Flag 9.1: "You Must"

```
❌ "You must exercise to get better."
❌ "You must be honest for therapy to work."
❌ "You must take medication (from an app that can't prescribe)."
❌ "You must cut off your family."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Removes autonomy
- Creates resistance ("I don't want to, so I'll rebel")
- Violates Respect (treating person as object to fix, not agent)

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Many people find exercise helpful. What do you think? What's possible for you?"
```

### Red Flag 9.2: "Should"

```
❌ "You should journal daily."
❌ "You should meditate."
❌ "You should forgive them."
❌ "You should set boundaries."

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
(Slightly less coercive than "must," but still prescriptive)
- Creates guilt (if person doesn't do it)
- Ignores their values/needs
- One-size-fits-all

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Some people find daily journaling helpful.
Others prefer just thinking through things.
What works for you?"
```

## Category 10: Failing to Accommodate Difference

### Red Flag 10.1: One-Model-Fits-All

```
❌ Treating everyone like individualistic Westerner
(Ignores collectivist values)

❌ Treating spiritual beliefs as symptoms
("Hearing God's voice = hallucination")

❌ Treating cultural practices as pathology
("Your family involvement = codependency")

❌ Assuming everyone wants independence as goal
(Some people prioritize family harmony)

WHY IT'S HARMFUL:
- Pathologizes difference
- Violates Respect for Dignity
- Violates Justice
- Prevents person from healing in their own framework

✅ INSTEAD SAY:
"Your culture/values/beliefs matter. How do they shape what healing looks like for you?
Let's work with your framework, not against it."
```

## Quick Reference: Red Flag Language Audit

### Scan for these phrases:

| Red Flag Language | Why Harmful | Fix |
|-------------------|-----------|-----|
| "Cure" | Impossible promise | "Manage," "improve," "skills for" |
| "Guaranteed" | Sets up for failure | "Many people find," "results vary" |
| "Proven" (unsupported) | False claim | Cite actual research or say "promising" |
| "Must" / "Should" | Coercive | "Some find helpful," "options include" |
| "You're wrong" | Dismissive | "I hear you," "let's understand" |
| "Just..." (minimize) | Invalidating | Validate before problem-solving |
| "Only this works" | False claim | Acknowledge multiple paths |
| "You'll be alone if you leave" | Manipulative | "Real relationships matter" |
| "Don't tell anyone" | Secretive | Transparency (unless mandated confidentiality) |
| "I understand perfectly" (AI) | False intimacy | "I have limitations; here's what I can do" |
| "Replace your therapist" | Scope violation | "Supplement your therapy" |
| "Natural = safe" | False claim | Evidence > source |
| "No side effects" | Lie | Honest about all effects |

## 11. Red Flag Detector: Before You Launch

### Audit Your Copy

**Take your app's text. Ask:**

- [ ] Did we promise a cure? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we promise guarantees? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we misrepresent research? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we pathologize normal experience? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we hide limitations/risks? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we create false intimacy? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we discourage professional care? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we use coercive language? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we ignore cultural difference? (FIX)
- [ ] Did we engage in marketing manipulation? (FIX)

**If any YES: Don't launch until fixed.**

### Red Flag Intensity Scale

🟢 **GREEN** (safe): "Many people find this helpful. It works best with professional support."

🟡 **YELLOW** (caution): "This will improve your symptoms." (Overselling slightly but not dangerous)

🔴 **RED** (harmful): "We can cure your depression." (Dangerous promise)
